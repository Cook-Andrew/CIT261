Pronouns

Pronouns in Esperanto are incredibly regular! Esperanto is all about consistent rules.

Possessive Case

In English we sometimes change the form of words like I to 'mine'. In order to minimize confustion, Esperanto always adds a suffix without changing the root word. To make a pronoun possessive, you add the suffix '-a'. The reflexive possessive case 'Si' is used to distinguish between objects belonging to the same person or a different person. For example, "He likes his dog" could mean he likes his own dog (li ŝatas sian hundon), or he likes someone elses dog (li ŝatas lian hundon).

Accusative Case

Accusative case is when something is the "target" of a sentence. For example, "I like them" rather than "I like they". In Esperanto, we add the suffix '-n' to the end. There is no reflexive accusative case (speaking in third person) difference: "myself" is just the same as "me"

Special Pronouns

The indefinate 'one' such as 'one must be dignified' is 'oni'. The equivalent to 'thou' is 'ci'. These will not come up terribly often, but it is wise for one to know them.
