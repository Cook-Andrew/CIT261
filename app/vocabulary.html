Vocabulary

Just about any word can be conjugated to become a noun, verb, or adjective; it just might not make sense. All nouns are conjugated with the "-o" suffix. For example, water is "akvo" and speed is "rapido". Verbs are conjugated by their tense as previously described. For example, watering is "akvas" and speeding is "rapidas". Adjectives end in the "-a" suffix. For example, watery is "akva" and fast is "rapida". Adverbs end in the suffix "-e". For example quickly is "rapide". You could technically conjugate water as an adverb but I am not sure how to use it in a sentence. To say something is the opposite of you add "mal-" to the beginning, such as "malbona" meaning "bad" (literally "not good"). Perhaps you could conjugate akve as "wetly", but that is more appropratly malseke (not dry).

To make a noun plural, add "-j" to the end. For example, "person" is "homo" and "people" is "homoj."

Most nouns are neuter, but to make a noun feminine add the suffix "-in" before the suffix "-o". For example, dog "hundo" can become "hundino".
