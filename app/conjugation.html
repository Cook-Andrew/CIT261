Conjugation

One of the reasons Esperanto is so easy, is because verbs follow the ideal of no exceptions to the rules. All verbs conjugate the same way!

Infinitive -i

Infinitive verbs are the unconjugated forms of verbs such as "to be" or "to want". When you use two verbs in a sentence, the second is infinitive. For example, "I wanted to do it".

  1. to be - esti
  2. to have - havi
  3. to do - fari
  4. to say - diri
  5. to go - iri

Present -as

While it is fairly obvious when the present tense should be used, it is worth noting that passive voice doesn't make any sense in Esperanto. In English we can say "The road is crossed by the chicken." much to the chagrin of English teachers, and it is technically right. In Esperanto you have to say "The chicken is crossing the road."

  1. being - estas
  2. having - havas
  3. doing - faras
  4. saying - diras
  5. going - iras

Past -is

  1. was - estis
  2. had - havis
  3. did - faris
  4. said - diris
  5. went - iris

Future -os

  1. will be - estos
  2. will have - havos
  3. will do - faros
  4. will say - diros
  5. will go - iros

Conditional -us

When we would do something it is conditional. For example, "I would like to speak Esperanto, but I must practice first." Remember not to use passive voice! Another use of conditional tense is sentences that begin with if. For example, "If you help me then I will learn Esperanto quickly!" technically should be "If you would help me then I would learn Esperanto quickly!"

  1. would be - estus
  2. would have - havus
  3. would do - farus
  4. would say - dirus
  5. would go - irus

Imperative - u

Imperative tense is often refrerred to as "command tense". For example, "Help me!" or "Go to church." In English we use unconjugated infinitives, but in Esperanto this is not the case.

  1. be - estu
  2. have - havu
  3. do - faru
  4. say - diru
  5. go - iru
